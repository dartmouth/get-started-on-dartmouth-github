Create a GitHub account for use at Dartmouth

Common scenarios

  1. I don't have a GitHub account and only need this for Dartmouth work
  2. I have a GitHub account for personal use, but I don't want to use that for my Dartmouth work

If you fall into (1), follow the steps below and sign up with your college email addresses (name@dartmouth.edu).

In the case of (2), you can also follow the steps below, but you can consider alternatively using your personal email. GitHub actually recommends this approach citing that a human-to-account is a one-to-one mapping (reference). For what it's worth, GitHub does a great job at letting you use one account and put on different personas depending on what you're doing. Also, when you leave the college it is easy/automatic to detach from your Dartmouth roles.

Steps

  1. Browse to https://github.com/
  2. In the top-right, click "Sign up"
  3. Enter your email (e.g. name@dartmouth.edu or handle@example.com)
  4. Enter a strong unique password (definitely different than your Dartmouth password)
  5. Enter a username (e.g. first-mi-last and anything of your choosing)
  6. Optionally opt-in to email from GitHub
  7. Click "Continue"
  8. You will likely now need to complete a challenge to confirm you are human
  9. After that, you will need to fill in a one-time code that is emailed to you
  10. Lastly, there are some questions about your team size and role, but you can also click "Skip personalization" at the bottom
Congratuations, you have a GitHub account!
